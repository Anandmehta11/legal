Child Custody Laws in India

Overview

Child custody laws in India are designed to ensure the welfare and best interests of the child. Here are some key points:

  • Custody can be granted to either parent based on the child's welfare.
  • Types of custody include physical custody, joint custody, and legal custody.
  • The court considers factors such as the child's age, emotional needs, and the parent's ability to provide care.

Key Laws

  • Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956: Governs custody for Hindus.
  • Guardian and Wards Act, 1890: Applicable to all religions.
  • Muslim Personal Law: Governs custody for Muslims.

Factors Considered by Courts

  • The child's preference (if old enough to express).
  • The financial stability of the parents.
  • The emotional bond between the child and the parent.
