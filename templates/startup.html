Startup Laws in India

Overview

Startup laws in India aim to promote entrepreneurship and innovation. Here are some key points:

  • Startups are eligible for tax exemptions under the Startup India initiative.
  • Intellectual property protection is provided to startups for trademarks, patents, and designs.
  • Startups can benefit from simplified compliance procedures and funding support.

Key Laws

  • Startup India Initiative: Provides tax benefits, funding support, and ease of doing business for startups.
  • Companies Act, 2013: Governs the incorporation and management of startups as private limited companies.
  • Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) Laws: Protect trademarks, patents, and copyrights for startups.

Steps to Register a Startup

  • Incorporate your business as a private limited company, LLP, or partnership firm.
  • Register with the Startup India portal.
  • Apply for tax exemptions and funding support.
  • Protect your intellectual property through trademark and patent registration.
