RERA Laws in India

Overview

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) was enacted to protect homebuyers and promote transparency in the real estate sector. Here are some key points:

  • RERA mandates the registration of real estate projects and agents with the regulatory authority.
  • It ensures timely delivery of projects and protects buyers from fraudulent practices.
  • Developers must disclose project details, including approvals, timelines, and financials.

Key Provisions

  • Section 3: Mandatory registration of real estate projects with RERA.
  • Section 4: Developers must maintain a separate escrow account for project funds.
  • Section 18: Buyers are entitled to compensation for delays or defects in the project.
  • Section 20: Establishment of a Real Estate Regulatory Authority in each state.

Steps to File a Complaint

  • Visit the RERA website of your state.
  • Fill out the complaint form with details of the issue and supporting documents.
  • Submit the complaint and pay the required fee.
  • Attend hearings and provide evidence as required.
