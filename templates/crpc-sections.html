Code of Criminal Procedure (CRPC) Sections

Overview

The Code of Criminal Procedure (CRPC) is the main legislation on procedure for criminal law in India. It provides the machinery for the investigation of crime, apprehension of suspected criminals, collection of evidence, determination of guilt or innocence of the accused person, and the determination of punishment of the guilty. Here are some key sections:

Key CRPC Sections

  • Section 41: When police may arrest without warrant.
  • Section 46: Arrest how made.
  • Section 49: Unnecessary restraint.
  • Section 50: Person arrested to be informed of grounds of arrest and of right to bail.
  • Section 54: Examination of arrested person by medical practitioner at the request of the arrested person.
  • Section 56: Person arrested to be taken before Magistrate or officer in charge of police station.
  • Section 57: Person arrested not to be detained more than twenty-four hours.
  • Section 125: Order for maintenance of wives, children and parents.
  • Section 144: Power to issue order in urgent cases of nuisance or apprehended danger.
  • Section 154: Information in cognizable cases.
  • Section 156: Police officer's power to investigate cognizable case.
  • Section 161: Examination of witnesses by police.
  • Section 164: Recording of confessions and statements.
  • Section 167: Procedure when investigation cannot be completed in twenty-four hours.
  • Section 173: Report of police officer on completion of investigation.
  • Section 190: Cognizance of offences by Magistrates.
  • Section 200: Examination of complainant.
  • Section 207: Supply to the accused of copy of police report and other documents.
  • Section 209: Commitment of case to Court of Session when offence is triable exclusively by it.
  • Section 300: Person once convicted or acquitted, not to be tried for same offence.
  • Section 311: Power to summon material witness, or examine person present.
  • Section 313: Power to examine the accused.
  • Section 319: Power to proceed against other persons appearing to be guilty of offence.
  • Section 354: Judgment.
  • Section 374: Appeals from convictions.
  • Section 437: When bail may be taken in case of non-bailable offence.
  • Section 438: Direction for grant of anticipatory bail.
  • Section 439: Special powers of High Court or Court of Session regarding bail.
  • Section 482: Saving of inherent power of High Court.

Importance of CRPC

The CRPC is crucial for providing a framework for the administration of criminal justice, ensuring fair trials, and protecting the rights of the accused.

Notable Chapters

Chapter V: Arrest of Persons

This chapter covers the procedures for arresting individuals, including when police can arrest without a warrant and the rights of the arrested person.

Chapter XII: Information to the Police and Their Powers to Investigate

This chapter deals with the process of providing information to the police about offenses and the powers of the police to investigate those offenses.

Chapter XIV: Conditions Requisite for Initiation of Proceedings

This chapter outlines the conditions that must be met before a court can take cognizance of an offense and initiate proceedings.

Chapter XXIX: Appeals

This chapter provides the procedures and rules for filing appeals against judgments and orders of criminal courts.
