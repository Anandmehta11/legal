Landlord/Tenant Laws in India

Overview

Landlord and tenant laws in India govern the rights and responsibilities of landlords and tenants. Here are some key points:

  • Rental agreements must be in writing and registered to avoid disputes.
  • Landlords cannot evict tenants without following due legal process.
  • Tenants are responsible for paying rent on time and maintaining the property.

Key Laws

  • Rent Control Acts: Protect tenants from arbitrary rent increases and eviction.
  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Governs the rights and duties of landlords and tenants.
  • Model Tenancy Act, 2021: Aims to balance the rights of landlords and tenants and promote rental housing.

Common Disputes

  • Non-payment of rent.
  • Illegal eviction by landlords.
  • Damage to property by tenants.
  • Disputes over security deposits.
