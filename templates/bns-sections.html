BNS Sections in India

BNS Section 189

An unlawful assembly is defined as a group of five or more persons with a common objective involving criminal force, such as intimidating governments or public servants, resisting law enforcement, committing crimes like mischief or trespass, unlawfully seizing property, or forcing someone to act against their legal rights.

Any individual who knowingly joins or remains in an unlawful assembly can face imprisonment of up to six months or a fine. If the assembly is armed or has been ordered to disperse by law, penalties may extend to two years of imprisonment.

BNS Section 24

In cases where an act is not an offence unless done with a particular knowledge or intent, a person who performs the act in a state of intoxication shall be liable as if they had the same knowledge as when sober. Exceptions exist if intoxication was administered without their knowledge or against their will.

BNS Section 49

Whoever abets any offence, if the act abetted is committed in consequence of the abetment, shall be punished with the same punishment provided for the offence, unless specific provisions exist.

BNS Section 176

Whoever, without written authority of a candidate, incurs or authorises expenses for holding public meetings, advertisements, or other actions to promote a candidate's election, shall be punished with a fine up to ten thousand rupees.

BNS Section 188

Whoever, without lawful authority, takes any coining tool or instrument out of any mint lawfully established in India, shall be punished with imprisonment for up to seven years and a fine.

BNS Section 180

Whoever possesses forged or counterfeit coins, stamps, currency notes, or banknotes, knowing them to be counterfeit and intending to use them as genuine, shall be punished with imprisonment for up to seven years or a fine, or both.
