Banking & Finance Laws in India

Overview

Banking and finance laws in India regulate financial institutions, transactions, and services. Here are some key points:

  • Banking laws govern the operations of banks, including deposits, loans, and investments.
  • Finance laws regulate financial markets, securities, and non-banking financial companies (NBFCs).
  • Key laws include the Banking Regulation Act, RBI Act, and SEBI Act.

Key Laws

  • Banking Regulation Act, 1949: Governs the functioning of banks in India.
  • Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934: Establishes the Reserve Bank of India as the central bank.
  • Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) Act, 1992: Regulates securities markets and protects investors.
  • Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881: Governs cheques, promissory notes, and bills of exchange.

Common Issues

  • Loan defaults and recovery.
  • Fraudulent banking transactions.
  • Disputes over interest rates and charges.
  • Non-compliance with financial regulations.
