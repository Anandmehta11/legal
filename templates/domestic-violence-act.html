The Protection of Women From Domestic Violence Act

Overview

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 is an Act of the Parliament of India enacted to protect women from domestic violence.

Key Features

  • Provides a definition of domestic violence that includes physical, sexual, emotional, and economic abuse.
  • Enables women to seek protection orders from the court.
  • Provides for the appointment of Protection Officers and Service Providers to assist victims of domestic violence.
  • Ensures the right of women to reside in their shared household, regardless of whether they have ownership rights in it.

Key Sections

  • Section 3: Definition of domestic violence.
  • Section 12: Application to Magistrate.
  • Section 17: Right to reside in a shared household.
  • Section 18: Passing of protection orders.
  • Section 19: Residence orders.
  • Section 20: Monetary reliefs.

Importance of the Act

The Act is crucial for providing legal protection to women facing domestic violence, ensuring their safety and well-being, and promoting gender equality.
