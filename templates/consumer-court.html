Consumer Court Laws in India

Overview

Consumer courts in India are established to resolve disputes between consumers and businesses. Here are some key points:

  • Consumer courts are governed by the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.
  • They handle cases related to defective goods, deficient services, and unfair trade practices.
  • Consumers can file complaints in district, state, or national consumer forums based on the claim amount.

Key Laws

  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: Provides a framework for resolving consumer disputes and protecting consumer rights.
  • Legal Metrology Act, 2009: Ensures accurate weights and measures in trade.
  • Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940: Regulates the sale and quality of drugs and cosmetics.

Steps to File a Complaint

  • Gather evidence such as bills, receipts, and communication records.
  • Draft a complaint mentioning the issue and relief sought.
  • File the complaint in the appropriate consumer forum.
  • Attend hearings and provide necessary documents.
