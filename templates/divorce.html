Divorce Laws in India

Grounds for Divorce

  • Cruelty
  • Adultery
  • Desertion
  • Conversion
  • Mental disorder
  • Irretrievable breakdown of marriage
  • Mutual consent divorce

Personal Laws

  • Hindu Marriage Act, 1955: Applies to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs.
  • Muslim Personal Law: Includes provisions like Talaq and Khula.
  • Christian Marriage Act, 1872: Governs divorce among Christians.
  • Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act, 1936: For Parsis.

Secular Law

  • Special Marriage Act, 1954: For interfaith marriages or those not covered by personal laws.

Key Aspects

  • Alimony/Maintenance: Financial support for the spouse post-divorce.
  • Child Custody: Decided based on the child's welfare.
  • Cooling-Off Period: Mandatory waiting period for mutual consent divorces, sometimes waived.
