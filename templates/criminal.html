Criminal Laws in India

Overview

Criminal law in India deals with offenses against the state or individuals. It includes provisions for punishment, rehabilitation, and deterrence. Here are some key points:

  • Criminal law is governed by the Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860.
  • Procedural laws include the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973.
  • Special laws exist for specific crimes, such as the Prevention of Corruption Act and the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act.

Key Sections of IPC

  • Section 302: Punishment for murder.
  • Section 376: Punishment for rape.
  • Section 498A: Protection of women from cruelty by husband or relatives.
  • Section 420: Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property.

Types of Crimes

  • Cognizable Offenses: Serious crimes like murder, rape, and robbery.
  • Non-Cognizable Offenses: Minor crimes like defamation and public nuisance.
  • Bailable and Non-Bailable Offenses: Based on the severity of the crime.
