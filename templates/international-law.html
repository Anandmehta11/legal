International Law

Overview

International law governs the relationships between nations and addresses global issues such as trade, human rights, and environmental protection. Here are some key points:

  • International law is divided into public international law and private international law.
  • It is enforced through treaties, conventions, and international organizations like the United Nations.
  • Key areas include human rights, trade law, environmental law, and conflict resolution.

Key Treaties and Conventions

  • United Nations Charter: Establishes the framework for international peace and security.
  • Geneva Conventions: Governs the treatment of prisoners of war and civilians during conflicts.
  • Paris Agreement: Addresses climate change and global warming.
  • World Trade Organization (WTO) Agreements: Regulates international trade.

Steps to Resolve International Disputes

  • Engage in diplomatic negotiations between the parties involved.
  • Seek mediation or arbitration through international organizations.
  • File a case in the International Court of Justice (ICJ) if necessary.
