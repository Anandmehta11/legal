GST & Tax Laws in India

Overview

GST (Goods and Services Tax) and tax laws in India regulate the collection of taxes by the government. Here are some key points:

  • GST is a unified tax system that replaced multiple indirect taxes like VAT, service tax, and excise duty.
  • Direct taxes include income tax, corporate tax, and capital gains tax.
  • Tax compliance is mandatory for individuals and businesses to avoid penalties.

Key Laws

  • Goods and Services Tax (GST) Act, 2017: Governs the levy and collection of GST on goods and services.
  • Income Tax Act, 1961: Regulates the collection of income tax from individuals and businesses.
  • Customs Act, 1962: Governs the levy of customs duties on imported and exported goods.
  • Central Excise Act, 1944: Regulates excise duties on manufactured goods.

Common Tax Issues

  • Non-compliance with GST filing requirements.
  • Disputes over tax assessments and refunds.
  • Tax evasion and penalties for non-payment.
  • Errors in tax returns and audits.
