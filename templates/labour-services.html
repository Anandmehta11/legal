Labour & Services Laws in India

Overview

Labour and services laws in India govern the rights and responsibilities of employers and employees. Here are some key points:

  • Labour laws ensure fair wages, safe working conditions, and social security for workers.
  • Services laws regulate employment contracts, termination, and dispute resolution.
  • Key laws include the Industrial Disputes Act, Minimum Wages Act, and Employees' Provident Fund Act.

Key Labour Laws

  • Industrial Disputes Act, 1947: Governs the resolution of industrial disputes.
  • Minimum Wages Act, 1948: Ensures minimum wages for workers in various sectors.
  • Employees' Provident Fund Act, 1952: Provides social security benefits to employees.
  • Factories Act, 1948: Regulates working conditions in factories.

Key Services Laws

  • Shops and Establishments Act: Regulates working hours, wages, and leave policies for employees in shops and establishments.
  • Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970: Protects the rights of contract workers.
  • Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972: Provides gratuity benefits to employees after retirement or resignation.
