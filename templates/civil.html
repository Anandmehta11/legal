Civil Laws in India

Overview

Civil laws in India govern disputes between individuals, organizations, or both. Here are some key points:

  • Civil laws deal with non-criminal matters such as property disputes, contracts, and family issues.
  • They aim to provide remedies like compensation or specific performance rather than punishment.
  • Key areas include property law, contract law, and family law.

Key Laws

  • Indian Contract Act, 1872: Governs contracts and agreements between parties.
  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Regulates the transfer of property between individuals.
  • Specific Relief Act, 1963: Provides remedies for breach of contracts.
  • Family Laws: Govern marriage, divorce, and inheritance matters.

Common Civil Disputes

  • Property disputes between individuals or organizations.
  • Breach of contract cases.
  • Family disputes such as divorce or inheritance issues.
  • Disputes over agreements or partnerships.
