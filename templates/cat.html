Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT)

Overview

The Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) was established under the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, to adjudicate disputes and complaints related to the recruitment and service conditions of public servants. Here are some key points:

  • CAT has jurisdiction over central government employees, excluding members of the armed forces.
  • It provides a speedy and cost-effective mechanism for resolving service-related disputes.
  • CAT decisions can be challenged in the High Court or Supreme Court.

Key Features

  • CAT operates independently of the regular judicial system.
  • It consists of judicial and administrative members with expertise in service matters.
  • CAT benches are located in various cities across India.

Steps to File a Case in CAT

  • Prepare a petition outlining the grievance and relief sought.
  • Submit the petition to the appropriate CAT bench.
  • Attend hearings and provide evidence to support your case.
  • Await the tribunal's decision or appeal if necessary.
