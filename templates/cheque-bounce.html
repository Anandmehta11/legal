Cheque Bounce Laws in India

Overview

Cheque bounce cases in India are governed by the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. Here are some key points:

  • A cheque is considered bounced when it is dishonored by the bank due to insufficient funds or other reasons.
  • The payee can file a legal complaint against the drawer under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.
  • Cheque bounce cases can lead to penalties, fines, or imprisonment for the drawer.

Key Laws

  • Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881: Governs cheque bounce cases and provides legal remedies for dishonored cheques.
  • Section 138: Specifies penalties for cheque bounce cases, including imprisonment up to 2 years or a fine.
  • Section 139: Presumes the liability of the drawer unless proven otherwise.

Steps to File a Complaint

  • Send a legal notice to the drawer within 30 days of receiving the cheque bounce memo from the bank.
  • If the drawer fails to respond or pay within 15 days, file a complaint in the appropriate court.
  • Provide evidence such as the dishonored cheque, bank memo, and legal notice.
