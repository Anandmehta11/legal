Medical Negligence Laws in India

Overview

Medical negligence refers to the failure of a healthcare provider to meet the standard of care, resulting in harm to the patient. Here are some key points:

  • Negligence can occur due to misdiagnosis, surgical errors, or improper treatment.
  • Patients can file complaints under consumer protection laws or approach medical councils.
  • Compensation can be claimed for physical, emotional, and financial damages.

Key Laws

  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: Allows patients to file complaints against negligent healthcare providers.
  • Indian Penal Code (IPC) Section 304A: Penalizes death caused by negligence.
  • Civil Procedure Code: Enables patients to file civil suits for damages.

Steps to File a Complaint

  • Gather evidence such as medical records and bills.
  • Consult a legal expert or consumer rights advocate.
  • File a complaint with the appropriate forum or court.
