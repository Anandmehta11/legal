Litigation and Dispute Resolution

Overview

Litigation and dispute resolution involve resolving conflicts through legal processes or alternative methods. Here are some key points:

  • Litigation refers to resolving disputes through the court system.
  • Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) methods include arbitration, mediation, and conciliation.
  • ADR is often faster, less expensive, and more flexible than traditional litigation.

Key Laws

  • Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996: Governs arbitration and conciliation proceedings in India.
  • Code of Civil Procedure, 1908: Provides the framework for civil litigation in courts.
  • Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987: Establishes Lok Adalats for resolving disputes through mediation.

Steps in Litigation

  • Filing a case in the appropriate court.
  • Serving legal notices to the opposing party.
  • Presenting evidence and arguments during hearings.
  • Receiving a judgment or decree from the court.

Benefits of ADR

  • Cost-effective and time-saving.
  • Confidential and less formal.
  • Preserves relationships between parties.
  • Provides flexible and creative solutions.
