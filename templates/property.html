Property Laws in India

Overview

Property laws in India govern the ownership, transfer, and disputes related to immovable and movable property. Here are some key points:

  • Property laws are governed by the Transfer of Property Act, 1882.
  • Other laws include the Indian Succession Act, Rent Control Acts, and Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act (RERA).
  • Disputes often arise in areas such as inheritance, tenancy, and property registration.

Key Laws

  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Governs the transfer of property between living persons.
  • Indian Succession Act, 1925: Governs inheritance and succession of property.
  • Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA): Protects homebuyers and regulates real estate projects.
  • Rent Control Acts: Protects tenants and landlords in rental agreements.

Common Property Disputes

  • Disputes over inheritance and wills.
  • Boundary disputes between neighbors.
  • Illegal possession or encroachment.
  • Issues with property registration or title deeds.
