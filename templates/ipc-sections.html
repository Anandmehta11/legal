Indian Penal Code (IPC) Sections

Overview

The Indian Penal Code (IPC) is the primary criminal code of India. It defines various crimes and prescribes punishments for them.

Key IPC Sections

  • Section 302: Punishment for murder.
  • Section 304: Culpable homicide not amounting to murder.
  • Section 307: Attempt to murder.
  • Section 309: Attempt to commit suicide.
  • Section 323: Punishment for voluntarily causing hurt.
  • Section 324: Voluntarily causing hurt by dangerous weapons or means.
  • Section 354: Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty.
  • Section 363: Kidnapping.
  • Section 375: Rape.
  • Section 376: Punishment for rape.
  • Section 377: Unnatural offences.
  • Section 379: Theft.
  • Section 392: Robbery.
  • Section 395: Dacoity.
  • Section 406: Criminal breach of trust.
  • Section 415: Cheating.
  • Section 420: Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property.
  • Section 494: Marrying again during lifetime of husband or wife.
  • Section 498A: Cruelty by husband or relatives of husband.
  • Section 499: Defamation.
  • Section 500: Punishment for defamation.
  • Section 506: Punishment for criminal intimidation.
  • Section 144: Unlawful assembly.
  • Section 34: Acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention.

Importance of IPC

The IPC is crucial for defining criminal offenses, ensuring uniform application of criminal law, and protecting individual rights while maintaining public order.

Notable Chapters

Chapter XVI: Offenses Affecting the Human Body

This chapter covers offenses such as murder, culpable homicide, kidnapping, and hurt. It deals with crimes that directly impact the physical well-being of individuals.

Chapter XVII: Offenses Against Property

This chapter includes offenses like theft, extortion, robbery, dacoity, and criminal misappropriation. It addresses crimes related to the unlawful taking or misuse of property.

Chapter XX: Offenses Relating to Marriage

This chapter covers offenses such as bigamy and adultery. It deals with crimes that disrupt the sanctity and legality of marriage.

Chapter XXII: Offenses Relating to Defamation

This chapter defines defamation and prescribes punishments for those who harm the reputation of others through false statements.

Special Provisions

Sections 108-120: Abetment and Criminal Conspiracy

These sections penalize abetment (encouraging or assisting in the commission of a crime) and criminal conspiracy (agreement between two or more persons to commit an illegal act).

Section 304B: Dowry Deaths

This section addresses dowry deaths, providing justice for victims and punishment for culprits involved in the death of a woman due to dowry-related harassment.

Section 144: Prohibiting Unlawful Assembly

This section prohibits unlawful assembly and is often invoked by authorities to maintain public order and prevent riots or disturbances.
