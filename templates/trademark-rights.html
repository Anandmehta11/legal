Trademark & Rights Laws in India

Overview

Trademark laws in India protect the intellectual property rights of businesses and individuals. Here are some key points:

  • Trademarks are symbols, logos, or names that distinguish goods or services.
  • The Trademarks Act, 1999, governs trademark registration and protection in India.
  • Trademark infringement can lead to legal action and penalties.

Key Laws

  • Trademarks Act, 1999: Governs the registration, protection, and enforcement of trademarks.
  • Copyright Act, 1957: Protects original works of authorship, including logos and designs.
  • Patents Act, 1970: Protects inventions and innovations.

Steps to Register a Trademark

  • Conduct a trademark search to ensure uniqueness.
  • File an application with the Trademark Registry.
  • Respond to objections or oppositions, if any.
  • Receive the trademark registration certificate.
