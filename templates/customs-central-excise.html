Customs & Central Excise Laws in India

Overview

Customs and central excise laws in India regulate the import, export, and manufacturing of goods. Here are some key points:

  • Customs laws govern the import and export of goods, including duties and tariffs.
  • Central excise laws regulate the manufacturing of goods and the levy of excise duties.
  • These laws aim to prevent smuggling, ensure compliance, and generate revenue for the government.

Key Laws

  • Customs Act, 1962: Governs the import and export of goods, including duties and penalties for violations.
  • Central Excise Act, 1944: Regulates the levy and collection of excise duties on manufactured goods.
  • Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992: Governs foreign trade policies and procedures.

Common Issues

  • Non-payment or evasion of customs duties.
  • Smuggling of goods across borders.
  • Disputes over classification and valuation of goods.
  • Non-compliance with excise regulations by manufacturers.
